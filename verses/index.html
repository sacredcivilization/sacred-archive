Quranic Verses

Key verses examined through Imami tafseer and the Akbarian school — two traditions that reach the same ontological conclusions from different starting points, united by Sayyid Ḥaydar Āmulī and Mullā Ṣadrā's explicit synthesis.

Imami Tafseer — al-Mīzān · Al-Kāfī · Akbarian School — al-Futūḥāt · Fuṣūṣ · Synthesis — Ḥaydar Āmulī · Mullā Ṣadrā
The synthesis tradition: Sayyid Ḥaydar Āmulī (720–787 AH) was the first to formally map Ibn ʿArabī's ontology onto Imami theology — arguing that waḥdat al-wujūd is identical to Imami tawḥīd, that the Insān al-Kāmil (Perfect Man) is the Imam of the age, and that Ibn ʿArabī's Khatm al-Awliyāʾ (Seal of Saints) is Imam ʿAlī. Mullā Ṣadrā continued this synthesis in his seven-volume Quranic tafseer. The verse pages below apply this combined lens: Shia bāṭin and Akbarian tajallī reading the same verse — and converging.

Q 5:55

إِنَّمَا وَلِيُّكُمُ اللَّهُ وَرَسُولُهُ وَالَّذِينَ آمَنُوا الَّذِينَ يُقِيمُونَ الصَّلَاةَ وَيُؤْتُونَ الزَّكَاةَ وَهُمْ رَاكِعُونَ

Āyat al-Walāya — The Foundation of Imamic Authority

The Quran's restriction of walāya to three parties: God, the Messenger, and the believer who gives zakāt in rukūʿ. Linguistic proof: innamā as ḥaṣr (restriction) — Q 5:55 affirms and simultaneously excludes. Shān al-nuzūl in Sunni sources (Ṭabarī, Rāzī, Qurṭubī, Nasāʾī): Imam ʿAlī's ring given in rukūʿ. Al-Kāfī: walāya is the fifth pillar; walāyatunā walāyat Allāh. Ibn ʿArabī: walāya kawniyya — giving from within the state of prayer defines the Quṭb. Q 5:55-57 as a closed unit: correct walāya (55) → victory (56) → prohibition of false walāya (57). Connection to Q 5:3 (Ghadīr) and Q 4:59 (ulī al-amr).

Q 2:30

إِنِّي جَاعِلٌ فِي الْأَرْضِ خَلِيفَةً

Adam as Khalīfa — The Origin of Walāya

The divine appointment of Adam as vicegerent: Imami ḥujja doctrine vs. Ibn ʿArabī's Insān al-Kāmil — and Ḥaydar Āmulī's proof that they are the same doctrine.

Q 4:59

أَطِيعُوا اللَّهَ وَأَطِيعُوا الرَّسُولَ وَأُولِي الْأَمْرِ مِنكُمْ

Ulī al-Amr — The Chain of Divine Authority

The most contested verse in Islamic political theology: who are the ulī al-amr? Imami proof — unconditional obedience can only be coherent if the ulī al-amr is infallible; Imam al-Ṣādiq's explicit identification in Al-Kāfī. Akbarian reading — ulī al-amr as the aqṭāb who carry the divine creative command. Ḥaydar Āmulī's synthesis: Imam and Quṭb are the same being. The Khawarij reading and why it destroys the authority chain Q 4:59 institutes.

Q 22:46

فَإِنَّهَا لَا تَعْمَى الْأَبْصَارُ وَلَٰكِن تَعْمَى الْقُلُوبُ

Hearts Blind, Not Eyes — The Bāṣīra Doctrine

Inner sight (bāṣīra) vs. outer vision: the Imam's teaching on the sealed heart vs. Ibn ʿArabī's organ of tajallī-perception — and why the Nawāṣib are the paradigm of spiritual blindness.

Q 33:33

إِنَّمَا يُرِيدُ اللَّهُ لِيُذْهِبَ عَنكُمُ الرِّجْسَ أَهْلَ الْبَيْتِ

Āyat al-Taṭhīr — The Purification of the Ahl al-Bayt

The Quran's declaration of the Ahl al-Bayt's ontological purification: Ṭabāṭabāʾī's grammatical proof of the verse's independence, ḥadīth al-Kisāʾ, Ibn ʿArabī on the maʿṣūm as Insān al-Kāmil, and Mullā Ṣadrā's proof that taṭhīr is an ontological statement — a divine act, not a moral commendation.

Q 57:3

هُوَ الْأَوَّلُ وَالْآخِرُ وَالظَّاهِرُ وَالْبَاطِنُ

al-Ẓāhir wa-l-Bāṭin — The Manifest and the Hidden

The Quran's most compressed divine ontology: Imam ʿAlī's Nahj al-Balāgha formulation (manifest not by sight, hidden not by subtlety), Ibn ʿArabī's waḥdat al-wujūd reading, and Mullā Ṣadrā's proof that ẓāhir/bāṭin is a distinction of ontological register — not space, not time.

Q 27:16

وَوَرِثَ سُلَيْمَانُ دَاوُودَ

Prophetic Inheritance — Warithat al-Anbiyāʾ

Solomon's inheritance from David as the Quranic template for prophetic succession: Fāṭima's Fadakiyya proof from this verse, Al-Kāfī's Imam-as-wārith doctrine, Ibn ʿArabī's wirāthat al-anbiyāʾ, and Mullā Ṣadrā's synthesis showing the walāya-chain and knowledge-chain are one.

Q 49:14

وَلَمَّا يَدْخُلِ الْإِيمَانُ فِي قُلُوبِكُمْ

Imān vs. Islām — The Interior Threshold

The Quran's own distinction between outward submission and interior faith: Imam al-Ṣādiq's two-threshold taxonomy in Al-Kāfī, Ibn ʿArabī's taslīm/mushāhada hierarchy, and Mullā Ṣadrā's proof that imān is an ontological event that enters the heart, not a belief-state the heart constructs.

Q 55:27

وَيَبْقَىٰ وَجْهُ رَبِّكَ ذُو الْجَلَالِ وَالْإِكْرَامِ

Wajh Allāh — The Face That Remains

The divine Face that endures when all else perishes: Al-Kāfī's identification of wajh Allāh with the Imam vs. Ibn ʿArabī's tajallī al-wajh — and Ḥaydar Āmulī's explicit equation of the two.

Q 24:35

اللَّهُ نُورُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ

Ayat al-Nur — The Light of the Heavens and the Earth

The Imam as the mishkat (niche) in which the divine light burns: Al-Kafi's identification of the lamp with prophetic knowledge, the glass with Imam Ali, and nurun 'ala nur as the unbroken chain of Imams transmitting the light. Ibn Arabi on Nur as Wujud. Haydar Amuli's synthesis: the Insan al-Kamil IS the mishkat.

Q 57:25

وَأَنزَلْنَا مَعَهُمُ الْكِتَابَ وَالْمِيزَانَ

The Mizan — Scripture and the Balance

The three instruments of every prophetic mission: clear signs, Scripture, and the Balance (Mizan). The Imam as Mizan al-Hayy (living Balance): nahnu al-mizan. Ibn Arabi on justice as ontological proportion. Mulla Sadra on the Mizan as the wujud-calibration principle of creation.

Q 21:105

أَنَّ الْأَرْضَ يَرِثُهَا عِبَادِيَ الصَّالِحُونَ

The Earth Inherited by the Righteous — The Quranic Basis of Raja

The divine inscription in the Psalms and the Reminder: the earth shall be inherited by the righteous servants. Al-Kafi: they are the companions of the Qa'im at the end of time. Mulla Sadra: divine promise is ontological necessity. The verse as the Quranic foundation of the Return doctrine.

Q 2:143

وَكَذَٰلِكَ جَعَلْنَاكُمْ أُمَّةً وَسَطًا

Umma Wasata — The Balanced Community

The community appointed as witness over humanity: Al-Kafi, Imam al-Sadiq — nahnu al-shuhada' (we are the witnesses). The Umma's balance derives from the Imam-witness at its center. Ibn Arabi on the wasata position as barzakh. Connected to Millat Ibrahimi and Q 21:105.

Q 63:1-4 · Q 4:145

إِذَا جَاءَكَ الْمُنَافِقُونَ قَالُوا نَشْهَدُ

Al-Munafiqun — Zahir Without Batin

The hypocrite who uses the highest truth-claim (shahada) as the deepest concealment. Al-Kafi: more dangerous than the open kafir — operates from inside. Ibn Arabi: the munafiq as the exact ontological inversion of the Insan al-Kamil (zahir deliberately opposed to batin). Mulla Sadra: Q 4:145's "lowest depths" is the ontological consequence of the most complete inner darkness.

Q 2:62 · Q 3:199 · Q 5:82

مَنْ آمَنَ بِاللَّهِ وَالْيَوْمِ الْآخِرِ وَعَمِلَ صَالِحًا

Ahl al-Kitab — Sincere Faith Across Traditions

Whoever believed in Allah and the Last Day and did righteous deeds — their reward with their Lord: Q 2:62's substance-over-form principle. The taqsir/qusur distinction. Q 3:199 — khashi'in lillah (humbly submissive) as the inner marker. Q 5:82 — la yastakbirun (no arrogance). Haydar Amuli: the walaya-chain's universal ontological reach.

Q 23:1-11

قَدْ أَفْلَحَ الْمُؤْمِنُونَ

Qad Aflaha al-Mu'minun — The Complete Mu'min

The seven qualities of the complete mu'min: khushu' (heart-presence in prayer) through faithfulness to trusts — as the inner content of what MILLAT-007 calls the "developed khudi." Al-Kafi: khushu' is hudur al-qalb (presence of the heart with Allah). Ibn Arabi: the seven qualities as the seven divine names properly reflected in the human heart. Mulla Sadra: falah as ontological state, not moral reward.

Q 36:51-53 · Q 75:1-6

وَنُفِخَ فِي الصُّورِ فَإِذَا هُم مِّنَ الْأَجْدَاثِ إِلَىٰ رَبِّهِمْ يَنسِلُونَ

Ma'ad — Resurrection and the Return of Wujud

The trumpet of Israfil: "at once they are from the graves to their Lord hastening" (ila rabbihim — the return is personal). Q 75 — nafs al-lawwama and the fingertips proof of complete individual resurrection. Ibn Arabi: the resurrection as the unveiling of the batin. Mulla Sadra's proof of bodily resurrection from al-haraka al-jawhariyya (the soul's substantial motion intensifying toward its divine completion).

Q 1:1-7

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ ۝ الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ

Surat al-Fatiha — The Complete Ontological Arc

Umm al-Kitab: all revelation compressed into seven ayat. The arc — from the origin of being (Basmala) through divine mercy (al-Rahman al-Rahim) through eschatological completion (Malik Yawm al-Din) to the mushahada pivot (Iyyaka Na'budu) to the three paths (al-mun'am 'alayhim / al-Maghdhub / al-Dallin). Nine propositions documented in T40. The Fatiha as the hub from which all other verse pages extend.

Q 4:165

لِئَلَّا يَكُونَ لِلنَّاسِ عَلَى اللَّهِ حُجَّةٌ بَعْدَ الرُّسُلِ

The Conclusive Proof — Lest People Have an Argument Against God

The Quranic ground of the luṭf principle: God sends messengers so creation has no valid ḥujja against divine accountability. Al-Kāfī — earth never without a Ḥujja (lest the earth itself have an argument against God). Ibn ʿArabī — prophetic mazhar continues through khatm al-awliyāʾ. Mullā Ṣadrā — ḥujja as ontological necessity for the actualization of human rational potential.

Q 7:12

قَالَ أَنَا۠ خَيْرٌ مِّنْهُ خَلَقْتَنِي مِن نَّارٍ وَخَلَقْتَهُ مِن طِينٍ

Anā Khayrun Minhu — The Iblīsic Structure of Refusal

The founding act of anti-walāya: Iblīs applies a material criterion (fire over clay) to a divine designation whose basis is comprehensive name-bearing (Q 2:31). Al-Kāfī: awwal man qāsa Iblīs — the first practitioner of qiyās against divine command. Imam ʿAlī's Khuṭba al-Qāsiʿa (Nahj 192): pride destroyed six thousand years of worship in a single moment. Ibn ʿArabī: frozen tawḥīd that affirms God while denying His tajallī in the mazhar. The structure repeated at every historical juncture where a divine naṣṣ was bypassed by self-referential calculation.

Q 5:67

يَا أَيُّهَا الرَّسُولُ بَلِّغْ مَا أُنزِلَ إِلَيْكَ مِن رَّبِّكَ

Āyat al-Tablīgh — The Divine Command to Declare Walāya

The command to declare walāya at Ghadīr Khumm: the conditional clause "if you do not, you have not conveyed the Message" places walāya at the apex of the entire prophetic mission. The divine protection clause (wa-Allāhu yaʿṣimuka min al-nās) as Quranic acknowledgment of political sensitivity. Ḥadīth al-Ghadīr narrated by 110 Companions (Aḥmad, Tirmidhī, Nasāʾī, Ḥākim). Q 5:67 + Q 5:55 + Q 5:3 as three verses of one declaration: definition → command → completion. Ibn ʿArabī: Ghadīr as the formal transmission of walāya kawniyya to the post-prophetic era.

Q 9:119

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا اتَّقُوا اللَّهَ وَكُونُوا مَعَ الصَّادِقِينَ

Kūnū Maʿa al-Ṣādiqīn — Be With the Truthful

The Quranic foundation of the silsila institution: al-ṣādiqīn with the definite article of specific reference; kūnū maʿa as a permanent imperative of state. Al-Kāfī: al-ṣādiqūna hum al-Aʾimma. The permanent imperative generates a permanent necessity: al-ṣādiqīn must exist in every age — Quranic grounding for the ḥujja doctrine. Ḥujwīrī: the Quranic authorisation for the murīd-shaykh relationship. Ḥaydar Āmulī: Imam, Quṭb, and Ṣādiq are three names for the same ontological station. Application to the age of Ghayba through Q 5:35 (wasīla) and the tawqīʿ of Imam al-Mahdī.

Q 4:145

إِنَّ الْمُنَافِقِينَ فِي الدَّرْكِ الْأَسْفَلِ مِنَ النَّارِ

al-Darku al-Asfal — The Hypocrites in the Lowest Depth

Why the munāfiq is below the kāfir: the kāfir's ẓāhir and bāṭin are aligned (consistent rejection); the munāfiq ruptures this correspondence (performed īmān over concealed kufr). Ṭabāṭabāʾī: Q 57:3 (huwa al-ẓāhiru wa-al-bāṭin) establishes God's reality as the supreme ẓāhir/bāṭin unity; nifāq is its most complete inversion. Q 9:101: munāfiqūn entrenched in nifāq existed within the Medinan community during the prophetic period — direct implication for universal ʿadālat al-ṣaḥāba. Ibn ʿArabī: frozen tawḥīd that denies tajallī in the walī is the most refined form of the ẓāhir/bāṭin split.

Q 3:199

وَإِنَّ مِنْ أَهْلِ الْكِتَابِ لَمَن يُؤْمِنُ بِاللَّهِ خَاشِعِينَ لِلَّهِ

Khāshiʿīna lillāh — The Honoured People of the Book

Three conditions for the honoured subset of Ahl al-Kitāb: dual-scripture faith, khushūʿ (interior humility), and non-bartering of divine signs. Divine guarantee: ajruhum ʿinda rabbihim. The taqṣīr/quṣūr distinction: khāshiʿīna as paradigm of quṣūr (incapacity, not culpable rejection). Al-Kāfī eschatology: Imam al-Mahdī judges Jews by the original Torah and Christians by the original Gospel; ʿĪsā (ع) prays behind Imam al-Mahdī, establishing Imamic precedence over prophethood at the eschaton. Q 4:159: all People of the Book will believe in ʿĪsā before his second death. The khāshiʿīna are most prepared for this final clarification.

Q 5:82-83

لَتَجِدَنَّ أَشَدَّ النَّاسِ عَدَاوَةً ۖ وَلَتَجِدَنَّ أَقْرَبَهُم مَّوَدَّةً

Ashadd ʿAdāwatan / Aqrab Mawaddatan — Enmity and Love

The structural heart-states that produce enmity and love: qissīsīn + ruhbān + lā yastakbirūn as the Christian formation nearest in love. Q 5:83 continuation: tears of recognition (mimmā ʿarafū min al-ḥaqq) as the fruit of the open heart. Structural reason for enmity: Q 2:89 taqṣīr — recognition followed by rejection. Al-Najāshī as the Sīra's illustration of Q 5:82-83. Ibn ʿArabī: kibr as the Iblīsic structure that seals the heart-mirror against tajallī. The axis: kibr (enmity) vs. khushūʿ (love) — connecting Q 5:82, Q 3:199, Q 7:12, Q 4:145 into one coherent doctrine of heart-states.

Q 33:72

إِنَّا عَرَضْنَا الْأَمَانَةَ عَلَى السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ وَالْجِبَالِ فَأَبَيْنَ

al-Amāna — The Trust Carried by the Human Being

The Trust offered to creation and accepted by the human being: Al-Kāfī — al-amānatu al-walāya (the amāna is walāya). Ẓalūman jahūlan as description of the weight assumed, not condemnation — the human accepted more than he fully grasped. Q 7:172 as the prior metaphysical grounding (the primordial covenant). Ibn ʿArabī: amāna as the full bearing of divine names — the heavens declined because they are partial reflections; the human being alone has the constitutional capacity to mirror all divine names. Ḥaydar Āmulī's synthesis: amāna = walāya = full divine name-reflection — three traditions, one station. The five-verse arc: Q 7:172 → Q 33:72 → Q 5:55 → Q 5:67 → Q 5:3.
