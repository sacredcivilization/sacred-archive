Imami Tafseer Al-Kafi (Kitab al-Hujja) · Tafsir al-Burhan (al-Bahrani) Vol. 4 · Bihar al-Anwar Vol. 23 · Tabatabai, al-Mizan Vol. 17 · Tafsir Nur al-Thaqalayn (al-Huwayzi)

The verse opens with thumma awrathna al-kitab — "then We caused to inherit the Book." Two words here demand attention before the three categories can be understood. First, thumma (then): the inheritance comes after something — after the prophetic mission of the preceding verse (Q 35:31: "What We have revealed to you of the Book is the truth"). The Book given to the Prophet (peace be upon him and his family) is then passed by God's own act to those He chose. Second, awrathna (We caused to inherit): the subject is God directly — this is divine inheritance, not human arrangement. The inheritance of the Book is not a matter of scholarly transmission or institutional succession — it is God's own designation of specific persons.

The recipients are alladhina istafayna min ibadina — "those We have chosen from Our servants." The word istafayna (chosen, selected, purified) is the same root as mustafa — one of the Prophet's titles. Al-Kafi (Kitab al-Hujja) records Imam al-Sadiq (peace be upon him) asked directly about this verse:

نَحْنُ هُمُ الَّذِينَ اصْطَفَانَا اللَّهُ مِن عِبَادِهِ ۖ وَأَوْرَثَنَا كِتَابَهُ ۖ وَالسَّابِقُ بِالْخَيْرَاتِ هُوَ الإِمَامُ

Al-Kafi, Kitab al-Hujja — Imam al-Sadiq (peace be upon him) on Q 35:32

"We are those whom God has chosen from His servants and caused to inherit His Book — and the sabiq bil-khayrat (foremost in good deeds) is the Imam." The identification is unambiguous: the mustafayn who inherit the Book are the Ahl al-Bayt. The three categories within them — zalim li-nafsihi, muqtasid, sabiq bil-khayrat — describe not outsiders but positions within the chosen community itself.

Tafsir al-Burhan (al-Bahrani, Vol. 4) records a narration from Imam al-Ridha (peace be upon him) with the fullest elaboration of the three categories:

الظَّالِمُ لِنَفْسِهِ مِنَّا مَن لَم يَعرِف حَقَّ الإِمَامِ ۖ وَالمُقتَصِدُ مَن عَرَفَ حَقَّ الإِمَامِ ۖ وَالسَّابِقُ بِالخَيرَاتِ هُوَ الإِمَامُ نَفسُهُ

Tafsir al-Burhan Vol. 4 — Imam al-Ridha (peace be upon him) on the three categories of Q 35:32

"The one who wrongs himself from among us is one who did not fully know the right of the Imam; the moderate one is one who knew the right of the Imam; and the foremost in good deeds is the Imam himself." The three categories are therefore internal to the Ahl al-Bayt community and its circle: the zalim li-nafsihi is not a disbeliever but one within the community who has not fully actualized walaya; the muqtasid is the committed follower; the sabiq bil-khayrat bi-idhni Allah — foremost by God's permission — is the Imam. The phrase bi-idhni Allah (by God's permission) is decisive: the Imam's being foremost is not a human achievement but a divine grant — which is precisely the meaning of isma (infallibility) and the divine designation (nass).

Bihar al-Anwar (Vol. 23) adds the connection to Fatima (peace be upon her) explicitly: the mustafayn chain — those chosen to inherit the Book — is constituted through her. Every Imam from al-Hasan (peace be upon him) onward is a sabiq bil-khayrat by God's permission, and every one of them is a son of Fatima (peace be upon her). The verse's declaration — dhalika huwa al-fadl al-kabir (that is the great bounty) — is the Quran's own evaluation: the inheritance of the Book through the Fatimid line is the supreme divine gift to creation, not a secondary blessing.

Tabatabai in al-Mizan (Vol. 17) grounds the verse's theological significance in the word awrathna itself: inheritance in Quranic usage implies the complete transfer of what the predecessor possessed, without diminishment. The Prophet (peace be upon him and his family) received the complete divine Book — not merely its text but its batin, its complete inner meaning, its living reality. The inheritance of the Book by the mustafayn is therefore not the inheritance of a text — it is the inheritance of the living knowledge that the prophetic heart contained. Tabatabai: "The inheritance of the Book is the inheritance of the complete divine knowledge — zahir and batin together — and this is what only the chosen (mustafayn) can carry."

The connection to Fatima's Fadakiyya sermon is direct: in that sermon, Fatima (peace be upon her) argued from Q 27:16 (Solomon inheriting David) and from Q 35:32 (the divine inheritance of the Book to the chosen) that prophetic inheritance is a Quranic principle, not a mere human custom. Her argument: if God Himself causes the chosen to inherit the prophetic legacy, then the denial of her inheritance from the Prophet is a denial of a Quranic institution. The verse is therefore both a theological statement and the Quranic ground of her claim.

Akbarian School Ibn Arabi, al-Futuhat al-Makkiyya Vol. 2 · Fusus al-Hikam (Fass of Muhammad) · Abd al-Razzaq al-Qashani, Tawilat al-Quran on Q 35:32

Ibn Arabi's reading of Q 35:32 in al-Futuhat (Vol. 2) centres on the verb awrathna and its recipient: "those We have chosen." The Akbarian framework identifies the inheritance of the Book with the inheritance of the complete ilm ladunni — the divine knowledge given directly from God's presence, not mediated through human study. This is the knowledge the Prophet (peace be upon him) received through revelation; the mustafayn who inherit it are those whose ontological constitution is capable of receiving and carrying this knowledge in its complete form — which is the definition of the Insan al-Kamil in each age.

The three categories (zalim, muqtasid, sabiq) map onto Ibn Arabi's ontological hierarchy of human beings relative to the divine self-disclosure (tajalli). The zalim li-nafsihi is the one whose faculty of reception is partially veiled — he wrongs himself by not fully opening to the tajalli that is available to him. The muqtasid is in balance — he receives the tajalli at his capacity. The sabiq bil-khayrat bi-idhni Allah is the Insan al-Kamil: the one who is foremost not by his own effort but bi-idhni Allah — by the divine permission that constituted him as the supreme mazhar. This is the Imam in Imami vocabulary — the one whose precedence is ontologically given, not earned.

Al-Qashani in Tawilat al-Quran: dhalika huwa al-fadl al-kabir (that is the great bounty) — the supreme divine gift is not material wealth, not political power, not even prophethood in isolation. The supreme gift is the inheritance of the complete Book — the living divine knowledge in its totality — and its installation in the Insan al-Kamil who can bear it completely. The Akbarian hierarchy of saints culminates in the one who inherits the complete Muhammadan light: the Khatm al-Awliya (Seal of Saints) who is, in Haydar Amuli's explicit identification, Imam Ali (peace be upon him) — whose line descends through Fatima (peace be upon her).

Synthesis — Haydar Amuli and Mulla Sadra Sayyid Haydar Amuli, Jami al-Asrar wa Manba al-Anwar · Mulla Sadra, al-Asfar al-Arbaa Vol. 4 · Mulla Sadra, Mafatih al-Ghayb

Haydar Amuli's synthesis in Jami al-Asrar addresses a question the verse itself raises: if the mustafayn who inherit the Book are the Ahl al-Bayt, and the sabiqun bil-khayrat are the Imams, what is Fatima's (peace be upon her) specific position in this structure? She is not counted among the twelve Imams in the juristic sense. Amuli's answer: she is the mother of the mustafayn — not herself the sabiq but the being through whom all the sabiqun receive their ontological constitution. The Imams are sabiqun bil-khayrat because they inherit the prophetic light through the Fatimid line; she is the channel of that inheritance. Without the mishkat (Fatima), the lamp of prophetic knowledge cannot reach the zujaja (the individual Imam). Q 35:32's inheritance is therefore Fatimid in its structure: God's act of causing the mustafayn to inherit the Book passes necessarily through Fatima (peace be upon her).

Convergence — Istifa, Wiratha, and the Fatimid Line

The Imami identification (Al-Kafi: mustafayn = Ahl al-Bayt; sabiq = the Imam) and the Akbarian identification (Futuhat: mustafayn = the Insan al-Kamil bearers of complete ilm ladunni; sabiq = the Khatm al-Awliya) converge on the same ontological claim: there is a continuous chain of human beings constitutionally chosen by God to carry the complete divine knowledge — not as scholars who acquire knowledge but as beings in whom divine knowledge is ontologically present. Q 35:32's dhalika huwa al-fadl al-kabir is the Quran's evaluation of this chain: it is the supreme divine gift to creation. Fatima (peace be upon her) is the constitutive mother of this chain — the being through whom all subsequent members of the mustafayn line receive their ontological constitution. Every Imam who is sabiq bil-khayrat bi-idhni Allah is a Fatimid Imam.

Mulla Sadra in al-Asfar (Vol. 4) provides the ontological clarification of what wiratha al-kitab (inheritance of the Book) means at the deepest level. For Sadra, the Book (al-kitab) is not primarily the written text — it is the divine amr (command/reality) that the text expresses. The mustafayn inherit not ink and paper but the living reality of which the text is an expression — the complete divine knowledge in its living, active form. This inheritance requires an ontological constitution capable of receiving it: the qalb (heart) that is the supreme mirror of divine self-disclosure. The sabiq bil-khayrat is the being whose heart is constitutionally the most complete mirror — and this is precisely Mulla Sadra's ontological description of the masum Imam. The great bounty (fadl al-kabir) is the divine gift of this constitutional capacity — given, not earned; designated (nass), not elected.
