Luṭf — The Divine Grace Obligation
اللطف · الواجب العقلي · أعظم الألطاف

Al-Ṭūsī's definition: luṭf is "what brings the morally obligated being closer to obedience without compulsion." Rational necessity (wājib ʿaqlī) derived from divine ʿadl + ḥikma. The Imam as aʿẓam al-alṭāf — greatest luṭf — combining living clarification, exemplification, and community-stabilization. Al-Ṭūsī's ghayba response: luṭf requires the Imam's wujūd, not ẓuhūr — physician exists though patient bars the door. Imami completion of Muʿtazilī luṭf argument (Q 2:124: imamate distinct from prophethood). Q 4:165 as the Quranic ground: messengers sent lest creation have a ḥujja against God.
